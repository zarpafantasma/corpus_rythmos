## 2. The Physics of Quantum Decoherence

### 2.1 The Current Bottleneck
In classical quantum computing (e.g., IBM, Google), qubits are incredibly fragile. Any interaction with the outside world—a stray photon, a slight temperature change, or ambient electromagnetic noise—causes the qubits to lose their superposition and entanglement. This is known as **decoherence**. 

To delay this, current quantum computers require massive, multi-million-dollar dilution refrigerators (often called "cryogenic chandeliers") to cool the processors to near absolute zero (milliKelvins). Even then, coherence only lasts for fractions of a second.

### 2.2 The RTM Topological Paradigm
Under RTM, thermal noise is just a symptom; the disease is topological diffusion (α < 0). The space-time fabric at standard conditions actively resists the preservation of quantum states over time.
