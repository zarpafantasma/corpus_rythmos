## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 The Vision

Quantum technologiesвҖ”computing, sensing, communicationвҖ”are limited by one fundamental enemy: **decoherence**. The fragile quantum states that enable exponential speedups and impossible measurements inevitably leak into the classical environment, destroying the very properties we seek to exploit.

Current approaches fight decoherence through:
- **Extreme isolation** (millikelvin temperatures, vacuum, shielding)
- **Error correction** (redundant qubits, syndrome measurement)
- **Faster operations** (complete computation before decoherence wins)

RTM proposes a radically different approach: **don't fight decoherenceвҖ”direct it**.

By engineering materials with topological gradients (вҲҮОұ), we may be able to create environments where decoherence is not suppressed uniformly, but **channeled directionally**вҖ”away from quantum information and toward designated "drain" regions.

## 10. Mathematical Framework

### 10.1 Modified Lindblad Equation

The standard Lindblad equation with Оұ-dependent rates:

```
GRADIENT-MODIFIED LINDBLAD EQUATION
в•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җ
