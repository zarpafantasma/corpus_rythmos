
### AI Interaction Guide: RTM Analysis Protocol
This document contains a structured sequence of "mega-prompts" designed to guide an Artificial Intelligence in the assimilation and auditing (Peer-Review) of the Corpus RyThMós (RTM). By dividing the analysis into 5 logical phases (from mathematical empiricism to human narrative), it prevents the AI from mixing contexts and ensures a scientifically rigorous analysis.

Technical Usage Notes:

Optimization: This prompt sequence was specifically designed and calibrated for Google Gemini 3.1 (Pro option and the best LLM model to date of this publication), leveraging its large context window and retention capacity.

Use with other AIs: If you decide to audit the corpus using other Large Language Models (LLMs), be very mindful of their limitations and strengths. Some platforms works better if you group and upload the documents for each phase as compressed files (.zip) rather than uploading the PDF´s one by one. Knowing the AI you are using is vital to ensure it doesn't lose the thread of the theory.

The entirety of the code and analysis was developed using Claude Opus 4.5 and Opus 4.6. If you want to run the simulations or real data analysis code, use these same Claude models or run the files (.py) directly on your computer, as other AI models may be unable to complete the process due to Execution Time Limitations. All instructions are in the main README on GitHub.

### General Considerations for AI Interaction

* **The "Golden Rule" of Context (001 & 002):** You can use almost any modern LLM to interact with individual PDFs from the corpus (for example, analyzing only the Ecology or Economics papers). However, to prevent the AI from misunderstanding the physics or hallucinating standard physical models, **you must always feed it the two foundational documents (001 and 002) first**. These contain the mathematical spine and the core invariant ($\alpha$) required for the AI to decode the rest of the work.
* **The Protocol is Optional:** The rigorous 5-phase "Red Team" protocol outlined above is designed specifically for comprehensive, peer-review-level auditing. However, it is not strictly mandatory if you simply want to explore specific parts of the theory.
* **The "Ask AI About RTM" File:** For a more conversational or guided exploration, you can optionally use the provided `AI_Discussion_Topics.md` file (located in the root directory next to this file you are reading.) . This document contains a curated list of thought-provoking questions and prompts designed to be asked *after* the 24 documents are fully uploaded into your AI of choice.
* **Accessibility of the Philosophical and Narrative Extensions (018–024):** It is important to note that while the first 17 papers demand a rigorous understanding of physics, scaling laws, and mathematical logic, the final section of the corpus (Documents 018 through 024) is designed to be entirely accessible to a general audience. You do not need a background in advanced mathematics or physics to engage with these texts. They explore the profound philosophical, anthropological, narrative, the human implications of the RTM framework, and the speculative nature of the Aetherion. If you are drawn to the overarching meaning, the storytelling, and the "why" of the theory rather than the underlying equations, you can dive directly into these final documents without the need for an AI application.
* **Context Window Limits:** Be mindful of the context window limits of your chosen AI. If you notice the AI beginning to lose track of the multiscale logic during long conversations, explicitly remind it to "re-evaluate the current topic strictly through the mathematical rules established in Document 001 and 002."
