## 1. The Classical Paradox Statement

### 1.1 The Traditional Formulation

The Grandfather Paradox, in its classical form, poses the following scenario:

> A time traveler goes back in time and kills their grandfather before their parent was conceived. This prevents the traveler's own birth, which means they could never have existed to make the trip, which means the grandfather was never killed, which means the traveler was born after all...

The result is an infinite logical loop with no stable resolution.

### 1.2 The Apparent Threat to RTM

At first glance, the Aetherion's capability for temporal manipulation appears to enable exactly this scenario. If we can navigate to "past" coordinates of alternative realities, what prevents us from navigating to the past of our *own* reality and creating paradoxes?

### 1.3 The RTM Answer

The answer is structural, not merely prohibitive:

> **You cannot reach your own past because your own past is part of your current reality's unique address, and that address cannot be tuned to without including your departure.**

The paradox is not prevented by a rule. It is prevented by the geometry of reality itself.

## 3. The Return as Re-Synchronization

### 3.1 The Mechanism of Return

When you "jump" to an alternative reality (Reality-B) and then decide to return to your origin, the Aetherion does not "travel" back in any conventional sense.

What it does is:

1. **Decouple** from Reality-B
2. **Re-tune** to the complete vibrational signature of Reality-A
3. **Re-couple** to that timeline

### 3.2 The Tuning Constraint

To tune into Reality-A, you must tune into the version of reality **where you have already left**.

You cannot tune into a version of Reality-A where you have not yet departed, because:
- That version does not have a distinct signature
- It is not a separate reality—it is a *segment* of the same reality
- The segment without your departure would be a different address entirely

### 3.3 The Only Valid Return

The only Reality-A you can return to is the one that **continued after your departure**. You re-enter the timeline at the point corresponding to your arrival, which is necessarily *after* you left.

## 4. Why You Cannot Reach Your Own Past

### 4.1 The Core Logic

Trying to return to your own past would be attempting to tune to a reality signature that:

- Includes all events up to a point before your departure
- Does NOT include your departure
- Does NOT include your life after that point

But Reality-A's signature is **holistic**. It is not composed of separable segments. The signature at any point includes the *entire causal history*, including future events relative to that point (which have already happened from the present perspective).

### 4.2 The Signature Includes Your Absence

Once you depart, Reality-A's signature **includes the fact that you are absent**. This is now part of what makes Reality-A uniquely Reality-A.

A "Reality-A where you are present at time T < T₀" would be a **different reality entirely**—one where you never departed. But that reality doesn't exist in the cascade, because you *did* depart.

### 4.3 The Reality Projector Cannot Play Deleted Scenes

Think of it this way: Reality-A is a complete recording. Your departure is edited into the recording permanently. You cannot ask the universe to play an alternate cut where you never left, because that cut was never recorded.

### 10.1 Signature Completeness Theorem

Let Σ(R) denote the vibrational signature of reality R.

For any reality R containing observer O:

```
Σ(R) = ∫_{t=-∞}^{t=now} H(R,t) dt

Where H(R,t) = complete history function at time t

If O departs at t = T₀:
    H(R, T₀) includes "O departed at T₀"
    
Therefore:
    Σ(R) includes O's departure
    
Any signature Σ' that does NOT include O's departure:
    Σ' ≠ Σ(R)
    
Therefore:
    Σ' corresponds to a DIFFERENT reality R' ≠ R
