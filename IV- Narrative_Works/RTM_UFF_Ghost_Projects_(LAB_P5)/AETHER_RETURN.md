## 2. Historical Context: Rise and Fall of the Classical Aether

### 2.1 The Necessity of a Medium

Pre-20th century physics operated under a seemingly reasonable assumption: waves require media. Sound waves propagate through air; water waves through water; mechanical waves through solids. When Maxwell demonstrated that light was an electromagnetic wave (1865), the question naturally arose: *through what medium do electromagnetic waves propagate?*

The luminiferous aether was proposed as this medium. Its required properties were extraordinary:

| Property | Requirement | Problem |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Rigidity | Must support transverse waves | Would impede planetary motion |
| Density | Must fill all space | Would affect gravitational calculations |
| Transparency | Must not absorb light | Contradicts material properties |
| Immobility | Must provide absolute reference | No experimental evidence |

### 2.2 The Michelson-Morley Experiment

In 1887, Michelson and Morley attempted to detect Earth's motion through the aether by measuring directional differences in the speed of light. The experiment used an interferometer sensitive enough to detect the expected "aether wind" caused by Earth's orbital velocity (~30 km/s).

**Result:** Null. No directional dependence was found.

This result admitted three interpretations:

1. The aether is dragged along by Earth (Stokes)
2. Objects contract in the direction of motion (Lorentz-FitzGerald)
3. There is no aether (Einstein)

### 2.3 Einstein's Resolution

Special relativity (1905) took the radical step of abandoning the aether entirely. Einstein's postulates:

1. The laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames
2. The speed of light is constant in all inertial frames

These postulates made the aether *unnecessary*. If light's speed doesn't depend on the observer's motion, there is no need for a medium that defines "absolute rest."

### 2.4 What Was Actually Rejected

It is crucial to understand precisely what special relativity rejected:

**Rejected:**
- A medium that defines absolute rest
- A substance through which Earth moves
- A preferred reference frame
- A mechanical carrier of electromagnetic waves

**NOT Rejected:**
- That the vacuum has properties
- That the vacuum has structure
- That the vacuum contains energy
- That the vacuum couples to fields

Einstein himself later acknowledged this distinction. In 1920, he stated:

> "According to the general theory of relativity, space is endowed with physical qualities; in this sense, therefore, there exists an ether. According to the general theory of relativity, space without ether is unthinkable."

The aether of special relativity's rejection was a *specific model*—the static, mechanical, frame-defining luminiferous aether. The *concept* of vacuum structure remained viable.
